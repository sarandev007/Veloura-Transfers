Important Information for WEF Davos 2026 Participants

During the World Economic Forum, strict security regulations, special access zones, and restricted routes apply in Davos. This overview helps you understand the most important rules and accreditations (AA, AB, hotel badges, Red Zone) ‚Äì ensuring smooth and compliant transfers.

Access Zones & Accreditations

With hotel stickers and driver badges, our chauffeurs may access secured hotels such as AlpenGold and Belv√©d√®re Steigenberger.

Hotel badges allow access through Promenade 101 (The Loft / Migros supermarket). From the Migros garage you can reach the Hilton Garden Inn and the Kongress Hotel.

Hotel stickers and driver badges do not grant access to the Restricted Access Zone (Red Zone).

The Red Zone includes areas such as Hertistrasse, the closed section of the Promenade near the Congress Center, the Kongress Hotel, and all entrances of the Congress Centre.

Access to the Red Zone requires AA or AB stickers ‚Äì these must be applied for directly by the guest through the WEF.

AA stickers (for governmental delegations) provide the highest access level, including Hertistrasse and the Kurpark Tunnel to the Congress Center.

The Davos Heliport is also only accessible with AA/AB credentials.

Rules for Waiting Zones & Hotel Access

In official waiting zones, chauffeurs must remain inside the vehicle and the engine must be switched off.

Hotel badges only allow drop-off & pick-up ‚Äì chauffeurs may not park or pick up guests inside the lobby.

If guests are not ready on time, chauffeurs must circle around the hotel again, which may take longer during the WEF.

Vehicles without accreditation must drop off guests farther away from the Congress Center or other WEF locations.
